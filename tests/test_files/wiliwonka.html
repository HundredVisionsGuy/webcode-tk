PREFACE

Homestuck is a webcomic primarily written and illustrated by Andrew Hussie. It ran from April 13, 2009 to April 13, 2016, with 8,123 total pages. It was originally hosted on mspaintadventures.com, but is better viewed via the very creative homestuck.com. It uses multiple mediums, from short gifs to 20-minute long animations to interactive games, complete with a lengthy soundtrack.

It's also Really Really Really weird.

Homestuck is a story about four 13 year-old kids who play a game that ends the world. A game in which said kids become gods. A game about time travel and multiverses and radioactive omniscient dogs. There's also gray space aliens but they don't even exist at the beginning and skipping right to them misses crucial worldbuilding and tone-setting.

In this, I'll just be going over some basics.

Chumhandle: A Pesterchum username. Typically following an “adjectiveNoun” structure.
Pesterchum: The fictional messaging application utilized by The Kids.
Text Color: Every character types or “talks” in a specific color.
Guardian: As in “parent or guardian.”
Specibus: The kind of weapon a character can wield. For example, bladekind allows you to use swords (and the like).
Captchalogue Modus: “Captchaloguing” is a cards-based* inventory system. A “modus” determines the rules of how said inventory works. A few modi are based off of computer data structures!
Sylladex: The name of one's inventory, i.e. “you captchalogue the HAMMER in your SYLLADEX.”

*The amount of items you can store in your inventory is, incredibly, determined by the amount of Captchalogue Cards in your deck/Sylladex.

FURTHER EXPLANATION ON TABLE DATA

1 = Chumhandle, 2 = Shirt Symbol, 3 = Guardian, 4 = Modus.

JOHN

  1. Shockingly, John likes Ghostbusters. “Really Terrible Movies” are kinda his thing.
  2. Ditto.
  3. Dad Egbert! Honest, hardworking father struggling to connect with his teenage son.
  4. Ah the Fetch and Stack moduses. Unequivocally horrible. Fetch utilizes a first-in last-out system, meaning you can only use the last card in your Sylladex; if you want to use a different item, you gotta get rid of that last one first.
    Stack is the same but using first-in first-out.

ROSE

  1. Rose is into scary things like The Eldritch Horrorterrors and psychoanalyzing her friends.
  2. A “Squiddle” is a goofy squid-like creature from a TV-show.
  3. Mom Lalonde. Absent, but rich. Rose views all of her mother's actions as passive-aggressive. But Mom really does just love wizards!
  4. The Tree modus is best explained by example. Say you have 3 items. A LAPTOP, VIOLIN, and GRIMOIRE. The first item you captchalogue will become the "root" card. If the LAPTOP is the root card, and you captchalogue the VIOLIN, it will sort into the right "branch" of the LAPTOP. This is because L > V; it comes first in the alphabet. Likewise, G > L, so the GRIMOIRE is sorted to the left branch.

(Using the root card will remove all items from your sylladex. Yikes!)

DAVE

  1. Dave is a Master of Sick Beats. (He has turntables!!)
  2. Ditto. Again.
  3. Bro Strider. Ninja puppet master. Scourge of Dave's life. You'll never see him coming.
  4. Like the Tree modus, the Hashmap modus is based off of the name of the item. Each letter in an item's name is assigned a value; 2 for consonants and 1 for vowels. It then adds up the numbers and divides by the number of cards in the deck. The final value is the remainder. For example, if you wanted to store some SHURIKENS, it would count S H U R I K E N S = 15. If we have 10 cards, we divide by 10 and get 1.5. The remainder is 5, so the SHURIKENS are stored in the 5th card.

JADE

  1. Jade has a garden! Well, a greenhouse, but close enough.
  2. The dog is because of Bec (see below), and the atom is because she's a Woman In S.T.E.M.!!! Hehe yeah she does science.
  3. Bec, full name Becquerel, is the radioactive dog I mentioned in the beginning. Jade had a Grandpa Harley but he's dead before the story begins.
  4. The Memory modus makes you match two cards to retrieve items. Pictionary makes you draw them.
